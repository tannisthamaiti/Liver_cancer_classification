Comparision to normal spectrum

Though the value of absorbency of the sick blood serum is almost equal to that of the normal blood serum in the most absorption spectra, there are some differences around 278 nm in the absorption spectrum. The absorbency of the blood serum with hyperglycemia is greater than that of the normal blood serum at 285 nm in the spectrum, and besides, there comes a peak shift of absorption with hyperglycemia.

Biomarkers of diseased spectrum

There are plenty of biomarkers in blood. The main objectives for Spectrum of blood analysis: to detect certain substances, to detect particular cells, and to detect whole blood. Among sample types, serum can retain the most substances in blood and is not subject to blood cell interference. Spectrum of serum albumin and globulin has been proved has prediction ability for hepatocellular carcinoma. The components in serum change to accompany the development of diseases, and these changes make a fingerprint-like identity for each individual sample.
